Conclusions

It turns out that Calvin & Hobbes and Garfield statistically have similar content. The top ten word categories for each comic are the same, and have generally the same percentage of occurrence

On top of that, it appears that Garfield is actually objectively more creative than Calvin & Hobbes. Garfield's content is 47% primary process, whereas Calvin & Hobbes' content is 44% primary process.

However, the differences in the result of content analysis were not statistically significant. From the results, we can conclude that both comics are actually similarly creative - from the perspective of naive content analysis.
